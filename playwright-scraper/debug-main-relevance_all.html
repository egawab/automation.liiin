Your board asks: "Is our marketing working?"

Most executive directors answer with activity metrics.
"We posted three times a week." "Our open rate was 22 percent." "We gained 200 new followers."

These are not wrong answers. They are just not answers to the right question.
The right question is whether your marketing is moving people — from unaware to aware, from aware to engaged, from engaged to donor, volunteer, or advocate.

If you cannot answer that, you do not have a marketing program. You have a marketing habit.

Here are the 5 metrics that actually tell you whether your nonprofit's marketing is working:
1. Qualified Audience Growth Rate
2. Engagement Rate
3. Conversion Rate by Persona
4. Cost Per Acquisition
5. Retention and Re-Engagement Rate

Most nonprofits track #1 and #2 and stop there. The ones closing the gap between activity and impact are tracking all five — and tying each one to a specific audience at a specific stage of the journey.

    A lot of people use “marketing” and “advertising” like they mean the same thing. They don’t. Marketing is the strategy behind the business. It’s about: • Understanding the audience • Building the brand • Setting the pricing • Choosing the right channels • Creating real value It answers: 👉 Why should people choose you? Advertising is the promotion side. It’s the campaigns, ads, and creative content designed to grab attention. It answers: 👉 How do we get in front of people? Here’s the reality: Great advertising can bring clicks. But weak marketing won’t keep customers. And great marketing without visibility? That stays invisible. The brands that win combine both: Strong strategy + smart promotion. One builds trust. The other drives attention. You need both to grow. 💬 What do you think businesses struggle with more today — marketing or advertising?
